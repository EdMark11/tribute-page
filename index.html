LeBron James

About

LeBron 'King' James is a professional basketball player in the National Basketball Association (NBA). He is often argued as the greatest basketball player of all time, alongside Michael Jordan. He has won 4 NBA Most Valuable Player awards and 4 NBA Championships with 3 different teams; Cleveland Cavaliers, Miami Heat (2) and Los Angeles Lakers. He has been to 10 NBA finals, including a dominant run of 8 consecutive appearances (2011 - 2018).

He is currently 2nd on the NBA all-time scoring list, on pace to beat the record in the 2022/2023 NBA season, and has the most points in the NBA Playoffs history. He has 18 NBA All-star appearances and holds the record for most NBA All-team selections at 17. LeBron finished the 2021/2022 NBA season averaging over 30 points per game, which is unheard of as prior to this feat the oldest players to achieve this average were 32 years old (Stephen Curry and Michael Jordan), this goes to show that not even father time can slow him down.

Career Timeline

  • 1984 - 30th December 1984, LeBron James is born in Akron, Ohio.
  • 1998 - 7th September, joins St. Vincent-St. Mary High School.
  • 2002 - Sports magazine 'Sports Illustrated' calls LeBron 'The Chosen One' as hype begins to build up.
  • 2003 - Declares for NBA draft and gets chosen as #1 pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers.
  • 2004 - Wins NBA Rookie of the Year and becomes the youngest player ever to win this award (at the time).
  • 2009 - Wins the NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) award.
  • 2010 - Wins the MVP award back to back. However, in the same off-season he announces that he will be joining the Miami Heat
  • 2012 - His decision to join the Miami Heat pays off as they manage to win the NBA finals, which is LeBron's first NBA Championship.
  • 2013 - Miami win championships in back to back years, and LeBron becomes the youngest player ever to score 20,000 career points.
  • 2014 - Announces that he will be returning to his former team, Cleveland Cavaliers.
  • 2016 - LeBron and the Cleveland Cavaliers wins the 2016 NBA finals, becomes the first team in history to come back from being down 3-1 in the finals to defeat the Golden State Warriors.
  • 2018 - Signs with the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 2020 - LeBron goes on to win his 4th championship.
  • 2022 - Becomes the oldest player in NBA history to average over 30 points per game in a season.
